Regional Tracking via ICGLR Database

The ICGLR (International Conference on the Great Lakes Region) has a wide range of remarkable features that make it an influential organization in Africa. First and foremost, its main objective is to promote peace, security, and stability among its member states, contributing to the development of the Great Lakes region.

Through a variety of mechanisms such as diplomatic negotiations and mediation processes, the ICGLR plays a crucial role in resolving conflicts and addressing issues such as political instability and human rights violations. Moreover, this regional entity champions economic integration by promoting trade facilitation, infrastructure development, and investment opportunities within member countries.

Additionally, the ICGLR prioritizes social issues through initiatives focused on gender equality, youth empowerment, and combatting sexual violence. Overall, this organization acts as a vital platform for cooperation and dialogue among countries in the Great Lakes region to address shared challenges effectively.

Regional Tracking via ICGLR Database

  • Regional Tracking of mineral flows via ICGLR database

    Data on regional mineral flows transmitted to ICGLR on a monthly basis.

    For each participant, data will be analysed to determine if exports equal legal imports.

    In case of imbalance, participant given a chance to explain and correct. If this is not done, participant will be declared non-compliant.

    All data and analyses will be stored on publicly accessible database.

    Price data not collected or stored or analyzed

    Audits Focus on Exporters

    Must be audited once per year

    the audit examines the exporter, and actors upstream in the mineral chain who handle, produce, process or supply minerals to the exporter

    exporter is responsible for his own compliance, and for that of his suppliers .

    Non-compliance by any of the upstream actors (not including mine site) is considered a noncompliance by the exporter.
